Narrative

Murray John Dowty
Born June 6, 1903. Presumed dead February 15, 1942.

Murray's birth was registered in the Registration district of Edmonton, sub-division of West Tottenham.
He was the firstborn & eldest son of Edgar John Dowty & Grace Marianne Parsons, who were married on February 14, 1903 at the Register Office, London City. Later in life Murray was upset to learn of the proximity of his birth date & his parents' wedding date &, particularly, that he had not been told.
He won a scholarship to London University, graduating BSc. Hons. (Lond), then joined the Malayan Civil Service (Electricity Authority).
A photo taken on August 6th,1928 shows him judging the tracking competition at the Selangor (Malaya) Scout Jamboree.
In late 1934 he visited his newly married brother Geoff & sister-in-law Essie in Merbein South, Australia.
An article in Blackwoods' Magazine (Feb., 1944?) describing the Japanese invasion of Singapore (Dec., 1941) refers to " The Perak River platoon under Lieutenant Murray Dowty was at Chendorah Dam. They got into position only a few hours before the Japanese attacked!!"
The story of their retreat is one of the British Army's gross mismanagement, ineptitude and lack of leadership; and of the courage of those who were messed around in the field. This story is told in the attachment to the letter to Lisa Dowty from the Ghurkha Museum, Winchester,of 5th October,1999. Tragic!
Murray was Mentioned in Despatches, published in the London Gazette, August 1st, 1946.
"(463) Dowty,2/Lieutenant M. John
25 Dec.1941 to 15 Feb.1942
2Lt(EC) 25 Dec.1941. Presumed dead 15 Feb.1942.
Before being commissioned he served in the ranks of the 1st (Perak) Battalion (FMSVF) and was in the Malayan Civil Service. He was appointed to the 2nd Battalion and served with it throughout the withdrawal to Singapore. On the night of 6th Feb 1942 and subsequent nights he, with a number of officers and 2nd Battalion men, daringly crossed the Straits of Johore and reconnoitred the Johore foreshore, and on return were able to report enemy activity. Shortly after surrender he and some other Malayan Volunteer officers asked for and were given permission to make a break.
His three companions were captured by the Japanese, but Dowty was never heard of again.
Medals - 1939-1945 Star; Pacific Star; War Medal 1939-45.
(FMSVF -Federated Malay States Volunteer Force)"

I recall as a boy going with my father to a patriotic night to boost morale in the Mildura Town Hall.This was either during the war (say 1944/45) or just after its end. Patriotic films were shown, speeches made and captured enemy war equipment, such as guns and even an aeroplane, were on display. The man running the show came up to Dad and said "So you made it", thinking he was Murray! Although they arranged to meet after the show we left early - Dad could not face it.
Murray's memorial is in the Kranji War Memorial for those many thousands who have no known grave - Section 320-321, on the second column on the right as one faces up the hill towards the curved memorial. "King Edward VII 2nd Ghurkha Rifles"
D.G.D. Feb. 2001
