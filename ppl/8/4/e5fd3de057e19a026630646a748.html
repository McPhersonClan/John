Narrative

Probably born at Phoness, his maternal grandfather's place; probably raised at Etterish until the family was forcibly removed when JamesMcPherson of Balavil bought the small properties of Invernahaven, Phoness & Etteredge (Etterish); raised his family between 1812 & 1826 at Baldow of Raits on the main Balavil estate; moved soon thereafter to Brae Ruthven on the other side of the Spey; remained there until at least 1851 and probably moved up Glen Truim to his childhood home at Etteridge after John, Donald etc emigrated to Australia.
"Baldow was part of the big three-davoch (twelve plough) land or farm of Raits just east of Kingussie on the north side of the Spey; its name signifies "the council hill or justice circle"; it is the site of the so-called Cave of Raitts which is actually a Pictish souterrain dwelling, the one which played a part in the early expansion of the Clan McPherson in Badenoch in the 15th century. .........Raitts was part of the mckintosh estate at a later date; it was also in the possession of the Macphersons of Banchor before they moved to Banchor; and at the end of the 18th century it was bought by James "Ossian" Macpherson with some other estates along the Spey and the Truim, on which he built the great mansion of Balavil on a fortune made in the servic (political) of the Indian princes in the British Parliament. ... Thomas must have been one of the tenants on the Raitts estate during the period 1812-1826". ( Alan G Macpherson to D.S. McPherson,1977)
